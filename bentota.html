About

Bentota, located on the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, is a tropical paradise known for its stunning beaches, lush greenery, and vibrant cultural heritage. This coastal town offers a perfect blend of relaxation, adventure, and cultural exploration, making it a popular destination for travelers.


Bentota's pristine beaches are the main attraction for visitors. With their golden sands, clear turquoise waters, and swaying palm trees, these beaches provide an idyllic setting for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports. Whether you want to soak up the sun, try your hand at surfing or jet skiing, or simply take a leisurely stroll along the shore, Bentota's beaches offer a range of activities to suit every traveler's preferences.

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

The hatchery serves as a sanctuary for various species of sea turtles, including the Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. It provides a safe environment for these turtles to lay their eggs, ensuring their successful hatching and subsequent release into the ocean.


One of the highlights of visiting the hatchery is the opportunity to witness the release of baby turtles into the sea. This awe-inspiring experience allows visitors to witness the beginning of these tiny turtles' incredible journey as they make their way into the vast ocean, navigating the waves and embarking on their lifelong adventure.


The Bentota Sea Turtle Hatchery also actively participates in research programs aimed at understanding the behavior and life cycles of sea turtles. By collecting valuable data and conducting scientific studies, the hatchery contributes to the overall conservation efforts and helps raise awareness about the importance of protecting these endangered species.

Day Excursions to Galle Fort & Bentota from Colombo


Galle, located in the southwest of Sri Lanka, is a vibrant coastal city that holds immense historical significance. It was once the bustling main port of the island until the Portuguese arrived in the 16th century. Today, Galle is celebrated as one of the country's most renowned beach destinations. As you explore this captivating city, keep an eye out for local fishermen selling their fresh catches along the roadside, while the breathtaking green and turquoise beaches that stretch along the coast captivate your senses. Trip with Stilt Fishermen


In the coastal village of Kosgoda, Sri Lanka, a captivating sight awaits visitors—the stilt fishermen. These skilled fishermen have mastered a unique fishing technique that has been passed down through generations. Perched precariously on wooden stilts, they cast their lines into the tranquil waters, patiently waiting for their catch. This age-old practice has become a symbol of the island's fishing heritage.

F.A.Q


What is Kosgoda famous for?

Kosgoda, located along the southwestern coast of Sri Lanka, is famous for its conservation efforts and its significant role in protecting sea turtles. This small coastal village has gained recognition as a prime destination for turtle conservation and serves as a sanctuary for these magnificent creatures.


What is the best time to visit Kosgoda?

The best time to visit Kosgoda, Sri Lanka is typically from December to March. During these months, the weather is generally dry and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities and beach exploration. The temperature during this period ranges from 25°C to 30°C, providing pleasant and comfortable conditions for travelers.
